Places to Visit Nearby

Peñón de Ifach Nature Reserve - One of the most striking landmarks in the whole of the Costa Blanca is the stunning 332m tower of the Peñon d'Ifach which towers over Calpe. You can climb to the top of one of the smallest natural parks in Spain. It is the symbol for the Costa Blanca although just looks like an immense bulk or lump of limy stone that reaches 332 metres in height and extends it arm into the sea measuring about 35 hectares. It was declared a Protected Natural Park in 1987 due to the great richness in flora and fauna that it holds. Over 300 species of flora can be appreciated and there is even one called the ‘sylene of Ifach’ which is only found there. As far as fauna, the coleopterous, silver seagulls and the swift are of great interest. If you are interested in serious climbing then click here.

Altea one of the most beautiful towns along the Costa Blanca and is only a 15 minutes drive south of Calpe along the N332. Famous for its church Nuestra Senora del Consuelo or Virgen de Consuelo with its fantastic blue roof. You can park in the twon square and climb to the top where the church is situated. Lots of nice restaurants and interesting little art shops along the way.

Moraira is a lovely little costal town with lots of restaurants. A 30 minute drive from Calpe along a pretty costal road, which winds along the cliff tops with stunning views of the Mediterranean.

Places to Visit Further Away

Benidorm, approximately 40 minutes drive south of Calpe along the N332 is the leading tourist enclave on Spain's Mediterranean coast. It has a complete range of facilities and services to accommodate the millions of national and foreign tourists each year. Skyscrapers, avenues and newly opened streets in this busy urban centre facing the sea have been created to offer a modern yet tasteful city for holidays and fun.

Benidorm Theme Parks and Water Parks. Benidorm boast some truly spectacular Theme Parks and Water Parks offering an amazing variety of entertainment

Guadalest is consistently ranked amongst the top tourist attractions in all Spain and it’s easy to see why. Approximately 30 miles inland south west of Clape on the CV-755 road, the small village has many artist’s workshops and houses carved into the living rock of the pinnacles high above the river which have some of the most spectacular views in the country.
